Festivals/Matching Events

Adoption Festivals
The Adoption Division of the Los Angeles County Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS) used to hold 3 – 5 "Adoption Festivals" every year. These festivals were designed to provide an informal, relaxed and playful environment in which to introduce pre-qualified prospective parents with hard-to-place children in the foster care system. In 2012, there were a total of approximately 185 families and 261 children that attended these festivals. Approximately 115 children were matched with families as a direct result of attending one of these. Successful matches have included up to 7 siblings being adopted by one set of parents, ensuring the siblings will grow up together in the same home. Locations are chosen and arranged by DCFS and are not open to the general public.

The Project Adopt festivals have been funded by Operation:Children since 1984 until 2013. While extremely successful in creating matches for children, older children fearing rejection did not want to participate. In 2013, DCFS decided to try a new method, similar to Kidsave , where they hold more, but smaller events at local venues around the county, now called “Matching Events”

Matching Events
Starting in 2013, Operation:Children now sponsors the DCFS Matching Events. In 2013, DCFS held 7 of these Matching Events. The premise is the same as Adoption Fests: Children and prospective prescreened adoptive families are invited to spend a few hours sharing activities and a meal together so that they can “get-to-know” each other better.

Most of these children are deemed “unadoptable” due to age, number of siblings, physical or mental disabilities – not something prospective parents put on paper that they want in a child. Somehow, meeting these kids in person puts a whole different light on it.
