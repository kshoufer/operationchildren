2010 World Arts Olympus, Long Beach CA
Hollywood legend, Jane Russell, lifetime active member of Operation: Children, was presented with the Golden Orb award on June 19, 2010. The award for Outstanding Humanitarian in the Arts was bestowed upon Jane for her tireless work to help children in need worldwide. Jane starred in films with the "Who's Who" of Hollywood, including Bob Hope, Frank Sinatra, Robert Mitchum , Clark Gable and in the classic film "Gentlemen Prefer Blondes" with Marilyn Monroe. In the middle of her illustrious career, she took the time to help orphaned children globally. She helped persuade the U.S. Congress to get laws passed to allow Americans to adopt orphaned refugee children from war torn countries. At the local level, Jane petitioned Los Angeles County with her idea of having hard-to-place children meet prospective parents in a park-like, festive environment. This adopt festival program is one of the major projects that Operation: Children still funds.

Kidsave Weekend Miracles Holiday Bash, December 10, 2011
Operation: Children sponsored another Kidsave Weekend Miracle's event (in partnership with DCFS) on 12/10/11. It was specifically for foster children ages 9 and older, who are in need of adoptive and/or host families. It was a fun day of holiday themed games and crafts for the kids along with great food. This event enabled the children to interact with prescreened prospective adoptive parents and host families. Operation: Children provided the funds and volunteered their time to help with the event. The event was not open to the public.

A Night in Monte Carlo, October 9, 2010 Fundraiser
In 2010 we held our annual fundraiser at the Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades. "A Night In Monte Carlo" featured dinner, dancing and casino action. It was a night to remember. Participating in our live and silent auctions provided additional fun and excitement, as we raised funds to support the Los Angeles County of Children and Family Services Project Adopt Festivals. Our 26th Anniversary Gala presented actor Willie Garson (White Collard, Sex and the City) with the first ever Jane Russell award. Also honored were Man of the Year, Mr. Danny Doyle and Ms. Amy Paffrath with the Outstanding Young Humanitarian award. The event was MC'd by the iconic Jeff Barry.
